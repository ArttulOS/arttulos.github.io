ArttulOS Artwork & Design Contributions

Back to Home

Visuals breathe life into any project, and ArttulOS is no exception! We warmly welcome your contributions to artwork, design elements, icons, wallpapers, and more. Your creativity can help shape the identity and user experience of ArttulOS.

Types of Contributions We Love

  • Original Artwork & Designs: If you're a digital artist, graphic designer, or illustrator, we'd be thrilled to see your original creations. This includes logos, icons, wallpapers, UI mockups, promotional materials, and any other visual assets.
  • AI-Generated Artwork & Designs: We embrace the use of AI tools for creating visuals! If you've used AI (like Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, etc.) to generate artwork or design concepts that fit ArttulOS, we encourage you to share them.

Keep it Neutral: Just like our code contributions, all artwork and designs must be free of political statements, symbols, or affiliations. Let's keep the focus on technology and community.

The Golden Rule: Proper Crediting

Whether your contribution is human-crafted or AI-assisted, transparent and accurate crediting is mandatory. This ensures fairness, respects intellectual property, and helps others understand the origin of the work.

  • For Original Designs:
    • Clearly credit yourself as the creator.
    • If your design incorporates any third-party assets (e.g., stock photos, specific fonts, icons you didn't create), you must ensure they are licensed compatibly for use in ArttulOS and provide proper attribution for these assets as required by their licenses.
  • For AI-Generated Artwork:
    • You must credit the AI tool(s) used (e.g., "Generated with Midjourney v6," "Created using Stable Diffusion XL").
    • If relevant and helpful, consider sharing key prompts or parameters used, especially for complex or distinctive results. This aids transparency and reproducibility.
    • You, as the contributor submitting the AI-generated piece, are responsible for ensuring, to the best of your ability, that the generated output does not overtly infringe on existing, distinct copyrighted works.

How to Submit Your Artwork & Designs

To streamline the contribution process and maintain good version control, all artwork and design submissions must be made via a public Git repository.

  • Accepted Platforms: You can host your repository on services like:
    • GitHub
    • GitLab
    • Gitea
    • Codeberg
    • Or any other publicly accessible Git hosting service.
  • Repository Contents: Your repository should include:
    • The artwork/design files themselves. We recommend:
      • SVG for logos, icons, and other vector graphics.
      • High-resolution PNG or JPG for raster graphics (e.g., wallpapers, complex illustrations).
      • Source files (e.g., .kra, .psd, .ai, .fig, .blend) are highly encouraged as they allow for easier modification and adaptation.
    • A README.md file at the root of your repository. This file must contain:
      • A brief description of the artwork/design.
      • Detailed crediting information (as outlined in "The Golden Rule" section above).
      • The license under which you are contributing the artwork (e.g., CC BY 4.0, CC0, MIT). Please choose a license compatible with the overall ArttulOS project goals. If unsure, ask a staff member.
  • Submission Process: Once your repository is ready, please contact an ArttulOS staff member or follow the project's established procedure for submitting external contributions (e.g., opening an issue with a link to your repository).

Your artistic contributions are incredibly valuable and help make ArttulOS more engaging and visually appealing. Thank you for sharing your talent and creativity with our community!
